# Beach Report App — Brief for Marketing Copy

## What the App Is
Beach Report is a mobile app (iOS & Android) that gives beachgoers real-time beach conditions so they can decide if it's a good day to head to the beach. It covers 200+ US beaches across all coastal states.

## Core Purpose
Help users plan their beach day with accurate, up-to-date safety and environmental data — all in one place, for free.

## Key Features

### Real-Time Conditions
- Air temperature
- Wind speed and direction
- Humidity
- Surf/wave height
- Water temperature

### Safety Information
- Color-coded beach flag system (green / yellow / red)
- Detailed safety alerts (storm warnings, dangerous surf, strong winds, cold water, high UV)
- Tapping the flag shows a full breakdown of current hazards

### Tides
- Current tide height and whether it's rising or falling
- Next 4 tide predictions with times and heights

### Sun & UV
- Sunrise and sunset times
- UV index with personalized sun protection tips (SPF recommendations, clothing advice, best times to be outdoors)

### Beach Discovery
- Search from 200+ US beaches
- Automatically sorts by distance using your location (GPS optional)
- Default popular beaches shown if location is not shared

### Favorites & Home Screen
- Save up to 5 beaches to your home screen
- Swipe between your saved beaches
- Heart beaches to add them to a favorites list

### Live Beach Cameras (select beaches)
- Link to live webcam feeds where available

## Data Sources (all free, public APIs)
- **Open-Meteo** — weather and wave/surf data
- **NOAA Tides & Currents** — tide predictions and water temperature
- **Sunrise-Sunset.org** — sunrise and sunset times

## Target Audience
- Beachgoers of all kinds: swimmers, surfers, families, tourists
- People who want a quick safety check before heading to the beach
- Coastal locals who visit beaches regularly
- Tourists visiting US beaches for the first time

## Platform
- iOS (iPhone)
- Android
- Built with React Native (Expo)

## Tone & Vibe
The app should feel friendly, reliable, and safety-conscious — not overly technical. It's for everyday beach lovers, not just surfers or weather nerds.
